Technical Overview

The MAYA Research Group conducts research at the intersection of artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and resilient system engineering, with a focus on protecting complex, large-scale digital ecosystems operating in adversarial environments. The group develops AI-driven security mechanisms for adaptive threat detection, autonomous response, and system self-healing across distributed, edge, and cyber-physical systems. Its research emphasizes robust and trustworthy AI, adversarial resilience, privacy-aware intelligence, and security-by-design architectures that enable systems to operate reliably under uncertainty, scale, and continuous attack.

MAYA is a Sanskrit term that denotes the intricate nature of reality and perception. For our research group, it carries a parallel significance: the intelligence required to understand, navigate, and secure the hidden complexities of modern computing infrastructures while designing resilient systems.

"To engineer autonomous, intelligence-driven computing systems that embed security at the core of distributed architectures, ensuring adaptive resilience, mission continuity, and data integrity in dynamic and adversarial environments."

Research Themes & Funding

Core Research Pillars

Machine Intelligence for Adaptive Defense

Behavior-driven AI security that replaces static signature-based detection with adaptive monitoring, enabling autonomous self-healing and reinforcement learning–based defense optimization.

Secure Distributed Systems & Edge Computing

Secure decentralized architectures leveraging blockchain-enabled trust and optimized edge-AI models to protect large-scale, heterogeneous IoT ecosystems.

Adversarial AI & Cyber-Deception

Active cyber defense mechanisms using high-fidelity deception environments and robust learning techniques to detect, mitigate, and prevent adversarial data poisoning and model manipulation attacks.
