Cognitive Computing simulates human thought, enabling systems to learn, reason, and selfcorrect from unstructured data using AI. Digital Twins are real-time virtual replicas of physical systems, providing monitoring, simulation, and optimization. The powerful convergence creates "Cognitive Digital Twins," where virtual models gain intelligence to autonomously learn, predict failures, and self-optimize their physical counterparts. This synergy is crucial for next-generation infrastructures, driving innovation in Smart Cities, digital health (Digital Human Twins), Industrial IoT, and agriculture. A dedicated journal section is vital to consolidate research in this rapidly evolving interdisciplinary field.

The topics of this section include but are not limited to:

  • Architectures and frameworks for cognitive digital twins.
  • AI/ML for predictive modeling and optimization in digital twins.
  • Real-time data integration and sensor fusion for digital twin updates.
  • Edge AI for localized cognitive processing in digital twins.
  • Security, privacy, and trust in cognitive digital twin ecosystems.
  • Smart city digital twins for urban planning and resource management.
  • Performance evaluation and metrics for cognitive digital twin systems.
  • Ethical considerations and explainable AI (XAI) in digital twin deployment.
  • ML and DL for advanced pattern recognition and decision-making in cognitive systems.
  • Reinforcement learning for autonomous learning and control in cognitive systems.
  • NLP and speech recognition for human-like interaction in cognitive systems.
  • Knowledge representation, reasoning, and semantic technologies in cognitive computing.
  • Cognitive robotics and autonomous systems.
  • Digital twin architectures, platforms, and interoperability standards.
  • Data management, storage, and security for digital twin ecosystems.
  • Lifecycle management of digital twins (design, deployment, operation, etc.)
